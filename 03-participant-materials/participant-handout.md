## What Is This Exercise?

You have been asked to participate in a tabletop exercise. This is a discussion-based walkthrough of a simulated cybersecurity incident. There are no computers involved, no systems being tested, and no technical actions to perform. You will simply talk through how you would respond to a scenario as it unfolds.

The facilitator will present the scenario in stages. At each stage, the group will discuss what actions to take, who is responsible, and what decisions need to be made.

---

## Ground Rules

1. **There are no wrong answers.** The purpose is to test our processes, not individuals. Be honest about what you would actually do.

2. **This is a safe space.** If you do not know the answer, say so. That is a valuable finding.

3. **Refer to our documents.** The Incident Response Plan, Escalation Decision Tree, and relevant policies are available during the exercise. Use them. That is exactly what they are for.

4. **Stay in character.** Respond as you would in your actual role during a real incident.

5. **One conversation at a time.** Let people finish their thoughts. The facilitator will manage the flow.

6. **Phones on silent.** Give this your full attention for 90 minutes.

7. **Confidentiality.** Details discussed during the exercise stay within the exercise group unless otherwise agreed.

## Quick Reference: Incident Severity Levels

| Severity | Examples | Response Time |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Critical | Ransomware across systems, mass data breach, EHR down | Immediate. Medical Director notified within 1 hour. |
| High | Confirmed account compromise with data access, malware on multiple devices | Within 2 hours. Operations Manager leads. |
| Medium | Single workstation malware (contained), suspicious sign-in activity | Within 24 hours. IT Administrator investigates. |
| Low | Phishing email reported (not clicked), single failed login attempt | Within 72 hours. Documented for trends. |

---

## Quick Reference: Pre-Authorized Containment Actions

The IT Administrator and Operations Manager can take these actions without waiting for Medical Director approval:

- Disable or suspend a compromised user account
- Force sign-out of all active sessions
- Reset a user's password
- Disconnect a device from the network
- Block a malicious email sender or domain
- Revoke suspicious third-party app access

---

## Quick Reference: Actions Requiring Medical Director Approval

- Notifying the Information Commissioner
- Notifying patients about a data breach
- Engaging external incident response support
- Contacting law enforcement
- Issuing any public or media statement
- Making ransom payment decisions (default position: do not pay)
- Shutting down a clinic location

## What to Expect

The exercise will follow this structure:

1. **Opening (10 min):** Facilitator introduces the exercise and ground rules
2. **Scenario walkthrough (60-75 min):** The facilitator presents the scenario in stages, pausing after each for group discussion
3. **Hot wash debrief (15-20 min):** Immediate discussion of what went well and what needs improvement

After the exercise, a formal After-Action Report will be compiled within 14 days with findings and recommendations.
